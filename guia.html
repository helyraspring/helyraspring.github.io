The 5-Step ATS Checklist
for Healthcare Workers

Everything you need to fix your resume and start getting callbacks — starting today.

Over 75% of healthcare resumes are rejected by Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) before a human recruiter ever sees them. This guide will walk you through 5 specific steps to make sure your resume passes the filter — and gets in front of real hiring managers.

1

Step One

Fix Your Resume Format

ATS systems scan your resume as plain text. Creative designs, tables, columns, and graphics are often unreadable — causing your resume to be rejected even if you're perfectly qualified.

  • Use a single-column layout — no tables or text boxes
  • Save your resume as .docx or .pdf (check the job posting for preference)
  • Use standard section headers: "Work Experience", "Education", "Certifications"
  • Remove graphics, icons, photos, and colored backgrounds
  • Use standard fonts: Calibri, Arial, Times New Roman, or Georgia
Quick test: Copy and paste your resume into Notepad. If it looks scrambled or loses formatting, an ATS will have trouble reading it.
2

Step Two

Add the Right Healthcare Keywords

ATS systems search for specific keywords from the job description. If your resume doesn't include them, it gets filtered out — even if you have the experience.

Clinical Skills

  • Patient assessment
  • Medication administration
  • Vital signs monitoring
  • IV insertion / IV therapy
  • Wound care
  • Patient education

Compliance & Systems

  • HIPAA compliance
  • EMR / EHR systems
  • Epic, Cerner, Meditech
  • OSHA standards
  • BLS / ACLS certified
  • Joint Commission

Soft Skills (ATS-friendly)

  • Team collaboration
  • Critical thinking
  • Care coordination
  • Patient advocacy
  • Clinical documentation
  • Time management
Pro tip: Copy 3-5 keywords from the exact job posting and add them naturally to your resume. ATS systems compare your resume to the job description word by word.
3

Step Three

Customize for Every Job

Sending the same resume to every employer is one of the most common mistakes. Each job posting has slightly different requirements — your resume needs to match each one.

  • Read the job description carefully and highlight key requirements
  • Match your resume summary to the specific role (RN, CNA, MA, Healthcare Admin)
  • Use the same job title from the posting in your resume header or summary
  • Add 3-5 keywords from the posting that aren't already in your resume
  • Adjust your top 3 bullet points to match the role's main responsibilities
Time-saving tip: Keep a "master resume" with everything included, then create a shorter customized version for each application. This takes 10-15 minutes per job — not hours.
4

Step Four

Write a Strong Professional Summary

Your professional summary is the first thing both ATS and recruiters read. It needs to include your role, years of experience, top skills, and 2-3 relevant keywords — in 3-4 sentences.

  • Start with your title and years of experience: "Registered Nurse with 5+ years of experience..."
  • Include 2-3 clinical keywords from the job posting
  • Mention your specialization (ICU, med-surg, pediatrics, etc.)
  • End with what you bring to the team or your career goal
Example: "Registered Nurse with 6+ years of experience in critical care and med-surg environments. Proficient in patient assessment, medication administration, and EMR documentation using Epic. ACLS and BLS certified. Committed to delivering compassionate, high-quality patient care in fast-paced hospital settings."
5

Step Five

Before You Send — Final Review

Before submitting any application, run through this final checklist. These small details are often overlooked but can make the difference between a callback and silence.

  • Spell-check everything — typos are instant disqualifiers
  • Check that your contact info (email, phone, LinkedIn URL) is correct and current
  • Make sure all dates are consistent and there are no unexplained gaps
  • Confirm your certifications are listed with expiration dates where applicable
  • Verify the file name is professional: "FirstName_LastName_RN_Resume.pdf"
  • Read it out loud once — this catches awkward phrasing your eyes skip over

Bonus: Your LinkedIn Profile Matters Too

Many US healthcare recruiters search LinkedIn before they even post a job. Here are the 4 changes that make the biggest difference:

  • Add your exact job title in your headline (e.g., "Registered Nurse | ICU | ACLS Certified") — this is what recruiters search for
  • Write an "About" section of at least 200 words that includes your top 5 clinical keywords
  • Upload a professional photo — profiles with photos receive 21x more views
  • Turn on "Open to Work" in your settings so recruiters can find you even if you haven't applied
